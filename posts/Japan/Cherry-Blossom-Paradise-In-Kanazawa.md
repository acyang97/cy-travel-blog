Kanazawa is a historic city on Japan's west coast, known for its cultural heritage, gardens, and traditional crafts. Its historic district has traditional buildings and landmarks, including Kanazawa Castle and Kenrokuen Garden. The city is famous for its gold leaf production, lacquerware, and Kutani porcelain. It seamlessly blends its rich cultural history with modern developments in art, architecture, and cuisine.

This was not my first time in Kanazawa. I went there for the first time back in 2016 before my national service. However, this time, I wanted to go there as part of my Sakura Hunting journey in Japan. As the Sakura season started much earlier in 2023 and I only reached Japan in 2 April, I have already missed the peak blossom in major cities like Tokyo, which was why I decided to immediately go to Kanazawa, since the [Sakura forecast](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2167.html) predicted that the Sakura season starts later there. Here are some of my favoruite Sakura places in Kanazawa

## 2. Kenrokuen Garden

Kenrokuen Garden is opposite Kanazawa Castle. Together with Kanazawa Castle during the spring time, lights are illuminated at night, making it is a beautiful place to view the Sakura in the night time. Also, it is free to enter for a week during the Spring period, it would usually cost 320 yen to enter the garden in other periods of the year!

## 3. Sai River

The Sai River flows along the south of Kanazawa City. It is a quiet place outside of the main attractions of the city, and you will find more locals here than tourists, something that I always appreciate.
